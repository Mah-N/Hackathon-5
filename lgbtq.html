    • LGBTQ+ Discrimination

    Definition

    LGBTQ+ discrimination refers to discrimination, prejudice, or harassment based on a person's sexual orientation, gender identity, or gender expression. It involves treating individuals or groups differently and unfairly because they are lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, queer, or otherwise not conforming to heteronormative norms.

    Examples of Discrimination

    1. Workplace

    i. An employee is denied a promotion solely because they are openly gay, despite being qualified for the position.

    ii. A transgender individual is subjected to offensive comments and jokes by coworkers, creating a hostile work environment.

    2. Housing

    i. A landlord refuses to rent an apartment to a same-sex couple, citing personal religious beliefs.

    ii. An LGBTQ+ individual is evicted from their apartment after the landlord discovers their sexual orientation.

    3. Education

    i. A transgender student is denied access to the restroom that corresponds to their gender identity, leading to feelings of exclusion and embarrassment.

    ii.A lesbian student is bullied by classmates, both verbally and physically, causing emotional distress and impacting their academic performance.

    Impact of Discrimination

    LGBTQ+ discrimination can have a profound negative impact on individuals and targeted groups, leading to:

    - Mental health issues, such as anxiety, depression, and suicidal thoughts.

    - Lower self-esteem and social isolation.

    - Economic hardship due to job loss or limited opportunities.

    - Reduced access to healthcare and other essential services.
